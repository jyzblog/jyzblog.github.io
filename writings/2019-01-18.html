Excerpts - One Minute Manager

January 17, 2019

<p>One Minute Goal Setting</p> <ul> <li>One minute goal setting: understand what the responsibilities are - 250 words, on the same page</li> <li>Problem solving: what is happening, what would you like to have happened? would doing A -> what you like to have happned to come true? then yes, good solution… complaining = not knowing what you’d like to have happened</li> <li>Iterate until figure out a solution..</li> <li>Never assume your employee knows anything with goal setting.. be extremely clear</li> </ul> <hr> <p>One Minute Praise</p> <ul> <li>Close monitor in the beginning, to "catch people doing something right"</li> <li>Tell upfront about what you are doing</li> <li>Praise immediatley, tell them how important they are to the organization, how good it makes you feel</li> <li>Stop for a moment of silence, encourage them to do the same</li> </ul> <hr> <p>One Minute Reprimand</p> <ul> <li>When someone did something significantly wrong <ul> <li>Asap</li> <li>Behavior not person</li> <li>On top of things</li> <li>"Fair"</li> </ul> </li> <li>1/2 reprimand: <ul> <li>Reprimand people immediately.</li> <li>Tell people what they did wrong—be specific.</li> <li>Tell people how you feel about what they did wrong—and in no uncertain terms. 5. Stop for a few seconds of uncomfortable silence to let them feel how you feel.</li> </ul> </li> <li>2/2 <ul> <li>Shake hands, or touch them in a way that lets them know you are honestly on their side.</li> <li>Remind them how much you value them.</li> <li>Reaffirm that you think well of them but not of their performance in this situation.</li> <li>Realize that when the reprimand is over, it’s over.</li> </ul> </li> </ul>
